# Manduka PRO — Why Serious Practitioners Pay $120 for a Mat

A budget yoga mat costs $20–30 and lasts 12–18 months of daily practice before compression loss
makes it feel like a thin carpet over hardwood. The Manduka PRO uses a 6mm dense, closed-cell
PVC foam with a lifetime guarantee and shows no measurable compression loss at 5 years.

## Closed-cell foam advantage

Open-cell foam mats (most budget options under $50) absorb sweat into the foam, creating hygiene
issues and breakdown over time. Closed-cell foam repels moisture — sweat stays on the surface
and wipes off. This matters for hot yoga and any practice that generates real sweat.

## Grip characteristics

The Manduka PRO has a "slick" surface when new that breaks in over 15–20 sessions. During break-in,
use a hand towel or yoga towel. After break-in, grip is consistent even in hot, sweaty sessions.
Budget mats often grip immediately but degrade after 6 months.

## The weight tradeoff

The PRO weighs 7.5 lbs — the heaviest mat we've tested at this thickness. It travels poorly.
For studio use, it's the best option. For travel, Manduka's eKO Superlite (2.2 lbs, $58) is the
complementary pick.
