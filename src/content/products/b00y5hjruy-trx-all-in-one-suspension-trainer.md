# TRX All-in-One — What You Can Actually Train With Straps

The TRX system handles push, pull, hinge, squat, rotation, and single-leg work — the six fundamental
movement patterns. It does not handle loaded barbell work, Olympic lifts, or heavy pressing beyond
bodyweight + leverage. Understanding this boundary determines whether it belongs in your program.

## Effective movements by category

**Upper body pull:** TRX Row, Face Pull, Bicep Curl — comparable to cable rows and machine curls.
**Upper body push:** TRX Push-Up, Chest Press — similar stimulus to push-ups with adjustable resistance.
**Core:** Fallout, Plank with Pike, Mountain Climber — outperforms floor exercises by adding instability.
**Lower body:** Squat, Lunge, Hip Hinge — good for rehab and mobility; limited for strength development above intermediate.

## The home anchor problem

The door anchor (included) fits most standard interior doors. Load rating: 350 lbs. For overhead anchor
(more natural pulling angle), ceiling mount hardware costs $15–20 and requires a joist anchor.
Failure to anchor into a structural point has caused injuries. Find a stud or joist.

## Portability value

The full system packs into a 2 lb bag the size of a water bottle. For travelers, it's the only
full-body training system that fits in a carry-on.
